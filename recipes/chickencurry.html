Chicken Curry

Description:

Filipino Chicken Curry is rooted in Indian cuisine, which was introduced to the Philippines during ancient trade routes. Spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and cardamom entered the local culinary scene. These aromatic ingredients formed the foundation for what woule eventually become one of Filipinos' favorite comfort foods. As with any traditional dish, Filipino cooks have always put their unique spin on recipes passed down through generations. To adapt curry to local palates, Filipinos added coconut milk for creaminess and sweetness while reducing the spice levels slightly compared to its Indian counterparts.

Every home cook has Pinoy-style chicken curry in their reportoire. The dish bears the DNA of Ginataang manok but what transforms it from ginataang manok to chicken curry is, curry powder. It is a dish that is earthy, spicy, warming, or even sometimes sweet. It is a well-balanced, richly spiced dish with notes of deep savory flavors from some of the ingredients like fish sauce, coconut milk, and evaporated milk. Preparation time can take up to 10 minutes while the cooking time can take up to 20 - 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • Curry Powder
  • Coconut Milk
  • Potato
  • Chicken
  • Carrots
  • Lemongrass
  • Bell Pepper
  • Fish Sauce
  • Onion
  • Ginger
  • Garlic

Steps:

  1. Firstly, we will add vegetable oil followed by ginger and garlic in the cooking pot.
  2. Next, we will add the chopped red onion which needs to be stirred till it turns translucent before adding the chicken leg.
  3. After that, we will add 1 tablespoon of curry powder which needs a quick stir.
  4. We will pour the water and let it boil for less than 5 minutes before pouring the coconut milk. Let it simmer for 30 minutes so the chicken can be cooked.
  5. Once the simmering reach in 10 minutes, add the salt, black pepper, and the remaining curry powder to the coconut milk-water stew.
  6. While waiting for the chicken to be cooked, We will pan-fry the potatoes and carrots in a separate pan. You can just half-cooked it since the rest will be done when you transfer it to the cooking pot.
  7. Once you already simmered for almost 25 minutes, add the bell pepper and half-cooked carrot to the cooking pot first. Let it cook for 5 minutes before adding the half-cooked potatoes.
  8. Stir gently and let it cook further till the chicken and vegetables are fully cooked.
  9. Add fish sauce since this is what gives the flavor of the curry stew. Serve it with rice and you are done.
